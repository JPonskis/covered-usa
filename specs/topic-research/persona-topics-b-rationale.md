# Persona Topics — Agent B (Coverage + Intuition) Rationale

**Template:** `/for/[persona]` — CoveredUSA persona pages
**Date:** 2026-05-15
**Approach:** Competitor enumeration + obvious-50 brainstorm + state-fork analysis
**Output:** 130 persona topics across 6 surface categories

---

## Methodology

1. WebFetch'd Stride Health blog (`blog.stridehealth.com`), HSAforAmerica blog, and Catch.co coverage. Searched NerdWallet, Healthline, GoodRx, KFF, BenAvest, FreelancersUnion, Worksuite, eHealth, SoloCovered, EntertainmentCommunityFund, TruckersHealthcare, FarmBureau, and HealthInsurance.org persona surfaces.
2. Brainstormed the obvious 60+ categories by enumerating: employment type, gig platform, life-stage, family-structure, status (unemployed/military/disabled), niche occupation, demographic/identity, health condition. Hit ~130 because the surface is deep.
3. Cross-referenced `busa-inventory.csv` — BUSA has heavy persona content but all SNAP/benefits-application angled. Zero direct health-insurance-for-X overlap. Flagged most as `slight` (BUSA = benefits-application intent, CoveredUSA = health-insurance-eligibility intent — formula separates them) or `none`.
4. Flagged `state_specific=y` on topics where state rules materially change the answer: AB5/Prop22 for rideshare in CA, NAR state license + brokerage rules for realtors, DOT for truckers, Farm Bureau for farmers, IHS for Native Americans, DACA/undocumented coverage variance, Medicaid coverage gap (10 non-expansion states), rural plan availability, reentry Medicaid by state.

## Competitor coverage map

**Stride Health:** Thin SEO surface. Blog has ~2 persona-explicit articles (1099 workers + Prop 22 stipend). Strong content via partner pages on DoorDash/Lyft sites, not their own SEO. They rely on app installs.

**Catch.co:** Even thinner SEO. Aggregates Healthcare.gov plans. No persona-page library. Brand recognition but not winning category search.

**HSAforAmerica:** Real blog. Persona pages: self-employed, small business owners, realtors, family-of-4, married couples, single moms (implied). ~6-8 explicit persona pages. Strong on HSA angle. CoveredUSA can beat them on subsidy math + state breadth.

**BenAvest:** Heavy self-employed + gig content. Two prominent pages. Strong on options breakdown but not state-specific.

**NerdWallet:** "Best health insurance for self-employed" + tax-deduction articles. Strong on comparison tables. Doesn't go deep on persona breadth — covers ~5 archetypes.

**Healthline:** Almost no persona-specific health-insurance content (their lane is medical info, not coverage shopping).

**GoodRx:** Self-employed + tax deduction + HSA + 2026 Big Beautiful Bill content. ~4-5 persona-adjacent pages.

**FreelancersUnion:** Insurance partnership pages, not SEO content. Community-oriented.

**HealthInsurance.org:** Strong persona FAQs ("how can gig workers get coverage") — these rank well. Covers ~15 personas at decent depth.

**SoloCovered:** Specialized blog for solo entrepreneurs. Has DoorDash, gig workers, musicians, influencers pages. Niche but growing.

**EntertainmentCommunityFund:** Sector-specific (artists/musicians/actors). Not SEO-optimized.

**TruckersHealthcare / TruckersNetwork:** Vertical-specific. Win their niche but tiny domain authority.

**RKAInsurance / Farm Bureau / KFF Health News:** Vertical or news coverage. Not SEO winners but topic-anchored.

**Verdict:** Persona category is the most fragmented competitive landscape across all 7 templates. No single competitor wins more than ~15-20 personas. CoveredUSA can plausibly own 80-100 personas with disciplined formula execution + AI-citation optimization.

## Niche occupations worth pursuing

These have low competitor density + strong commercial intent + clear FAQ patterns:

1. **Realtors** — 1.5M NAR members, 87% are 1099, variable income, no brokerage coverage. RKA + HSAforAmerica own but small DR.
2. **Truckers (owner-operators)** — vertical sites only. Strong subsidy-cliff impact in 2026.
3. **Farmers / Ranchers** — KFF Health News covers but news angle, not buying guide. 2026 premium quadrupling for some farmers is a story.
4. **Musicians / Actors / Artists** — EntertainmentCommunityFund owns the niche but no SEO. SoloCovered has thin pages.
5. **Travel nurses** — multi-state coverage challenge, no clear category winner.
6. **Content creators / influencers / Twitch streamers / YouTubers / OnlyFans** — modern fast-growing personas, almost no specialized content. SoloCovered + HealthBasedHealthcare are the only competitors.
7. **Construction trades (electricians/plumbers/carpenters)** — SmartandSimple owns but generic.
8. **Clergy / missionaries / church employees** — GuideStone + denomination-specific, no general guide.

## Topics deliberately excluded

- **Generic "health insurance for adults"** — too broad, no persona angle.
- **Brand/carrier-specific personas** ("BCBS for freelancers") — carrier comparison fits a different template.
- **Pure tax-deduction articles** — these belong in a Q&A or glossary template, not persona.
- **State Medicaid eligibility per state** — overlaps with Track D Medicaid factory (separate template).
- **Procedure or drug × persona** — those are procedure/drug template with persona modifier in FAQ.
- **Pure "best plan" comparisons** — Q&A template territory.

## Surprises

- **OnlyFans creators** is a real persona with growing search demand and zero authoritative content. SoloCovered is the only competitor.
- **Twitch streamers / content creators / influencers** — surprisingly thin coverage given creator-economy size.
- **2026 subsidy cliff** creates a new high-value persona ("Above 400% FPL") that didn't exist as a clean category pre-2026.
- **DACA + undocumented state-fork** is suddenly urgent given the CA Medi-Cal phase-out + MD pilot. Almost no content addresses this with current rules.
- **Adjunct faculty / contingent academic workers** — large population (~750K adjuncts in US) with almost zero dedicated content. AAUP partial coverage only.
- **Foster youth aging out** — Medicaid-to-26 provision is hugely underexposed. Niche but real.
