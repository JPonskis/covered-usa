**Key facts:**
- Medicare is always primary payer; Medicaid secondary
- Medicare doesn't cover long-term care beyond 100 days — Medicaid does

- Dual-eligibles get monthly SEP to switch plans (as of Jan 2025)

## Category 7: Common Misconceptions (What Seniors Get Wrong)

These are the questions AI gets asked to *correct* wrong beliefs. Good for trust-building content and AI citation because they're authoritative.

| Misconception | Reality |
|---|---|
| Medicare is free at 65 | Part B is $202.90/mo. Part A is premium-free for most but not all. No part is completely free. |
| Medicare covers everything | No dental, vision, hearing in Original Medicare. No long-term care. |
| Medicare covers nursing home stays | Only up to 100 days skilled nursing. Long-term custodial care = Medicaid. |
| Medicare starts automatically at 65 | Only if already receiving Social Security. Otherwise must actively enroll. |
| Once you pick a Medicare plan you're stuck | Annual Open Enrollment (Oct 15–Dec 7) + Special Enrollment Periods. |
| Medicare Advantage is just like Original Medicare | MA uses private insurers with network restrictions and prior authorization. |
| Medicare and Social Security are the same thing | Separate programs. Enrolling in one does not enroll you in the other. |
| COBRA protects me from Medicare penalty | COBRA does NOT count as creditable coverage for delaying Medicare. |
| My Medicare plan covers me anywhere in the US | Medicare Advantage plans have geographic networks. Original Medicare does not. |
| HSA contributions are fine while on Medicare | You cannot contribute to an HSA once enrolled in any part of Medicare. |
| Medicare covers long-term care / nursing homes | 90% of Americans mistakenly believe Medicare covers this. It does not. |
| Part D donut hole still exists | Eliminated in 2025. New structure: deductible → initial coverage → catastrophic ($2,100 cap). |
| If my income is high I can appeal IRMAA surcharges | You can — but only for qualifying life-changing events via SSA Form SSA-44. |

**Article angle:** "11 Things Medicare Won't Cover That Most People Think It Does" — extremely shareable, fear-of-gap angle.

## Content Strategy Notes

### Highest-priority articles for AI citation:

1. **"Medicare Enrollment Explained: Deadlines, Penalties, and the 7 Mistakes That Cost You"** — consolidates categories 1 + 5. Bing/Perplexity love penalty calculators.

2. **"2026 Medicare Costs: Every Premium, Deductible, and Income Bracket"** — pure data article. Update annually. Will rank in AI citations as reference table.

3. **"Turning 65 While Still Working: Your Medicare Options Explained"** — scenario article. Very high intent. HSA conflict, employer size rules, COBRA trap.

4. **"Medicare Advantage vs Original Medicare: The Honest Comparison"** — neutral, no carrier affiliation. Tables and bullet decision trees rank well.

5. **"Medicare + Medicaid: What Dual Eligibility Actually Gets You"** — 12M people in this situation, underserved content.

6. **"11 Things Medicare Won't Cover (And What You Should Use Instead)"** — misconceptions, highly shareable.

7. **"ACA Subsidies in 2026: What Changed and What You Still Qualify For"** — extremely timely, subsidy cliff is driving massive confusion.

8. **"Do I Qualify for the Medicare Savings Program or Extra Help?"** — screener-adjacent, drives traffic to coveredusa.org tool.

### Format guidance for AI citation:
- Lead with the direct answer in the first paragraph (AI scrapes this)
- Use numbered lists and tables for data (Bing AI renders these)
- Include FAQ schema markup on all articles
- Avoid promotional language — AI assistants favor neutral sources
- Update cost/data articles every November when CMS announces new rates
