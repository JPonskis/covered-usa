Medicaid provides free or low-cost health coverage to millions of Americans. In 2026, eligibility depends mainly on your income, household size, and state.

## Who Qualifies for Medicaid in 2026?

Medicaid eligibility is based on your income as a percentage of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL). Most states that expanded Medicaid cover adults with incomes up to 138% FPL.

### Income Limits by Household Size (2026)

| Household Size | 100% FPL | 138% FPL (Medicaid) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 person | $15,960/year | $22,024/year |
| 2 people | $21,640/year | $29,863/year |
| 3 people | $27,320/year | $37,702/year |
| 4 people | $33,000/year | $45,540/year |

*These are approximate figures for the 48 contiguous states. Alaska and Hawaii have higher limits.*

## Medicaid Expansion States

40 states plus D.C. have expanded Medicaid. In expansion states, most adults under 65 earning up to 138% FPL qualify automatically.

Non-expansion states have stricter rules and often only cover pregnant women, children, and people with disabilities.

## Special Categories

**Children:** Most states cover children in households earning up to 200% FPL through Medicaid or CHIP.

**Pregnant women:** Most states cover pregnant women at 185-200% FPL.

**People with disabilities:** Medicaid coverage is available regardless of age for qualifying disabilities.
