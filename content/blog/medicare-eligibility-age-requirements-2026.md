Medicare is the federal health insurance program for people 65 and older, and for certain younger people with disabilities. Here's everything you need to know about qualifying in 2026.

## Who Qualifies for Medicare?

### Age-Based Eligibility (65+)
You automatically qualify for Medicare Part A (hospital coverage) at age 65 if you or your spouse worked and paid Medicare taxes for at least 10 years (40 quarters).

### Disability-Based Eligibility (Under 65)
If you're under 65, you can qualify for Medicare if you:
- Have received Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) for 24 months
- Have ALS (Lou Gehrig's disease) — coverage starts immediately
- Have End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) requiring dialysis or a kidney transplant

## Medicare Parts Explained

| Part | Coverage | Cost (2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Part A | Hospital stays | Free for most people |
| Part B | Doctor visits, outpatient | ~$185/month premium |
| Part C | Medicare Advantage plans | Varies |
| Part D | Prescription drugs | Varies |

## When to Enroll

Your Initial Enrollment Period (IEP) is 7 months: starting 3 months before your 65th birthday month and ending 3 months after.

If you miss your IEP and don't have employer coverage, you may face a late enrollment penalty.
