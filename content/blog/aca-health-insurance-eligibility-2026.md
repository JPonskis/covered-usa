The Affordable Care Act (ACA) marketplace offers subsidized health insurance to millions of Americans. In 2026, premium tax credits help reduce your monthly costs based on income.

## Who Qualifies for ACA Subsidies in 2026?

To qualify for ACA premium tax credits, you generally need to:

1. **Income between 100% and 400% FPL** — See the table below
2. **Not eligible for Medicare or Medicaid**
3. **Not have affordable employer coverage** (defined as costing more than 9.02% of income)
4. **Be a U.S. citizen or lawfully present immigrant**
5. **Enroll during Open Enrollment or a Special Enrollment Period**

## ACA Subsidy Income Limits (2026)

| Household Size | 100% FPL | 200% FPL | 300% FPL | 400% FPL |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 person | $15,960 | $31,920 | $47,880 | $63,840 |
| 2 people | $21,640 | $43,280 | $64,920 | $86,560 |
| 4 people | $33,000 | $66,000 | $99,000 | $132,000 |

Subsidies scale down as income increases. Households at 100-150% FPL typically qualify for the most generous subsidies.

## Open Enrollment Dates

ACA Open Enrollment runs November 1 through January 15 each year. After January 15, you can only enroll if you have a qualifying life event (job loss, marriage, birth of a child, moving, etc.).

## What If I Qualify for Medicaid Instead?

If your income falls below 138% FPL and you live in a Medicaid expansion state, you'll be directed to Medicaid instead of the ACA marketplace. Medicaid is free with no premiums.
